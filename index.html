About

I am a third year Ph.D. student passionate about leveraging programming and data science to explore fundamental questions in evolutionary biology. My research interests span evolutionary theory, population genetics, genomics, and machine learning, with a particular focus on recombination rate evolution. I enjoy developing computational approaches to analyze large-scale biological data and am especially excited by the potential of machine learning to uncover patterns in evolutionary processes. For me, the intersection of biology and computation offers endless opportunities to solve complex, fascinating puzzles.
As I am nearing the end of Ph.D., I am iterested both academic and industry post-doctoral positions where my computational skills and biology knowledge can contribute to innovate research.
